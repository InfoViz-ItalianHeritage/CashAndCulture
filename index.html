Picking the data

We started by extracting the number of projects and their funding details from the Opencoesione datasets available and split according to years. Also extracting participation in cultural events from the dataset available

  • Compile a count of projects and their funding
  • Compile people's participation in cultural events
  • Determine which policies influenced cultural participation

Data Cleaning

In this step, we extract all relevant data and transform them into useable data structures to be visualized using python libraries

  • Removing any duplicate entries from the tables.
  • Changing the data formats of some columns (date using the format Day-Month-Year).
  • Merging tables that contain yearly data.
  • Separating the type of funding received by cultural institutions (public funding, private funding).

Graphing Data

To answer the research questions, graphs are created to showcase trends and highlight useful figures:

  • The regions with the highest funding received for cultural heritage projects
  • The regions with the highest participation in cultural heritage projects and events
  • The trend of funding and how it changed through the years 2014-2024

Research Questions

How do cultural funding policies in Italy influence public participation in cultural institutions over time and across regions?

Sub-question 1

Which types of cultural institutions receive the largest share of funding, and how does this relate to their visitors' number?

Sub-question 2

Has cultural funding across Italian regions remained stable or fluctuated over the years 2014-2027, and what trends can be observed in public participation during the same period?

Sub-question 3

Does higher household spending on culture reinforce or counterbalance the effect of public funding in driving participation?
